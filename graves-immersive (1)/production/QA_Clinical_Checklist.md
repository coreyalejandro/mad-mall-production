# Clinical QA Checklist
Use for review before picture lock.

## Thyroid
- Diffuse enlargement around trachea (goiter), not nodular disease
- TSI antibodies bind TSH receptor; T3/T4 surge visualized as hormone flow
- Accurate butterfly geometry and anterior neck location
- No thyroiditis or surgical imagery

## Heart
- Tachycardia representation (100–120 bpm) without ischemia or necrosis
- Emphasis on beta-adrenergic sensitivity; optional beta-blocker metaphor
- No alarming or gore visuals

## Brain & Nerves
- Pituitary **suppresses TSH** in hyperthyroidism (shown clearly)
- Anxiety/insomnia cues appropriate; no pathologizing language
- Tremor within physiologic band 8–12 Hz

## Eyes (Graves’ orbitopathy)
- Mild proptosis, lid retraction, tear-film dryness; tasteful depiction
- Improvement path indicated; no surgery/gore

## Skin/Hair/Scalp
- Diffuse thinning vs. patchy alopecia; sweating/itch without rash gore
- Hair regrowth suggestion after normalization; lighting preserves melanin fidelity

## Muscles & Bones
- Proximal muscle weakness signs; protein catabolism metaphor
- Bone: increased resorption > formation pre-treatment; partial recovery post-treatment

## Tone & Accuracy
- Awe and curiosity—not fear; no pain grimaces
- Terminology correct; no overclaiming cures
- Captions/VO clinically aligned with visuals
