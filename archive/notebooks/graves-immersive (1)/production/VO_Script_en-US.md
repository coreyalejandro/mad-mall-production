# VO Script (en-US)
Rate: ~150–160 wpm; Warm, confident tone; Neutral American English (or African diaspora narrator)

## 01 — Title (14s)
Inside a Black woman’s body with Graves’ disease: a guided tour. With dignity and curiosity, we’ll journey from the surface into living systems—seeing how the thyroid, heart, brain, eyes, skin, muscles, and bones respond—and how healing begins.

## 02 — Welcome (14s)
Graves’ disease is common in women and affects Black people more often. This is your body’s story—clear, compassionate, empowering. We’ll move inside the bloodstream toward the thyroid, to watch what’s happening and why you feel what you feel.

## 03 — Thyroid (16s)
Autoantibodies bind the thyroid’s receptors and push it into overdrive. Hormones surge—turning up metabolism, warmth, and sweat. The gland enlarges and glows like a furnace. These signals race through the blood, touching every system you live in.

## 04 — Heart (14s)
Your heart beats fast and strong, extra-sensitive to adrenaline. Palpitations can feel loud—but it’s your body keeping pace. Treatments like beta-blockers help the rhythm settle while thyroid-calming care begins to take effect.

## 05 — Brain & Nerves (16s)
In the brain, nerves fire quickly. Anxiety rises, sleep can fracture, hands may tremble. The pituitary lowers TSH to slow the thyroid, but antibodies keep pressing “go.” With care, this cascade can quiet—and calm return.

## 06 — Skin, Hair, Scalp (14s)
Warm, damp skin and itch reflect the metabolic heat. Hair can thin or shed. Follicles are resilient—when thyroid levels normalize, regrowth follows. Surface changes echo the storm beneath, and they ease as balance returns.

## 07 — Eyes (14s)
Inflammation behind the eyes can gently push them forward and dry the surface. Lubrication, protection, and time help—the active phase plateaus and improves. Vision is precious; your body works to safeguard it.

## 08 — Muscles & Bones (16s)
Muscles tire and weaken as protein breaks down; bones lose density when turnover speeds up. Treatment helps reverse the trend—fibers strengthen and bone rebuilds. Shaken, but strong, your body is ready to rebound.

## 09 — Resilience (14s)
As the thyroid calms, systems harmonize. Heart rate steadies, jitters ease, sleep returns, skin cools, hair regrows, eyes feel better. Your body is not your enemy—it’s been trying to protect you all along.

## 10 — Conclusion (12s)
With knowledge and care, this chapter becomes one of strength. Your body is your ally. Healing is underway.

Recording spec: 48 kHz, 24-bit WAV; mic 15–20 cm; -18 LUFS raw; de-ess, light compression (2:1).
